Spotlight on the Streets: Meet Rokko Tuah, Berlin’s Hidden Gem

Beneath the towering facades of Mitte and the cobbled streets of Kreuzberg lies an untold story, splashed across concrete walls and rusty alleyways. Rokko Tuah, a rising star in Berlin’s vibrant street art scene, is quietly making waves with their unique style and evocative creations.

Who is Rokko Tuah?

Little is known about Rokko Tuah’s personal life. Their work, however, speaks volumes. Described as a fusion of surrealism and urban expressionism, Rokko’s art often features haunting faces, intricate geometric designs, and bold, emotive messages.

A Hidden Talent in Plain Sight

Rokko’s pieces are scattered across lesser-known corners of Berlin: a tucked-away mural in Neukölln, a striking installation on the side of an abandoned factory in Wedding. Fans are starting to document these pieces on social media with the tag #RokkoTuah.

Why Rokko Tuah Matters

Berlin has always been a canvas for renegades and revolutionaries. Rokko Tuah’s work captures the raw, unfiltered essence of the city—a counter-narrative to gentrification and commercialization.
